My UX Playbook

The Mission


To create a new understanding of exceptional;   winning consumer loyalty through the simplicity, coherence and beauty of our digital products.

The Process

  1. Discover
  2. Define
  3. Design
  4. Prototype
  5. Test

DISCOVER

Design should be fearless in its quest for understanding.

Design ensures that a perfect focus is held at all times on the user, the business, and the most important objectives and goals to be achieved by the design system.

DEFINE

Design should always carefully consider the context within which it lives.

Context is ever evolving, shaped by constant physical, emotional, rational, cultural, social, technological and other interventions.

Strong design accommodates and responds to each specific context.   Our job is to translate raw, unstructured research into clear, actionable and contextual insights.

DESIGN

Design should express the value of having been expertly and thoughtfully constructed.

Craftsmanship is how much attention, and intention, and care we put into our design.

In a world where a premium is placed on speed, this may seem oxymoronic.  But explicitly expressing craftsmanship in our design is key to rising above the average, and key to earning the respect of our clients and our customers.

PROTOTYPE

Design should be testable by humans at the earliest possible opportunity.

Design systems cannot prosper without change.   As designers we embrace a mindset of actively and purposefully creating units of design that can be placed in front of humans for feedback as quickly and as often as possible.

TEST & ITERATE

Design should be an iterative process of growth and of new understandings.

We use the awareness gathered from testing to inform how best to evolve our design system to deepen its relevance and connection with the customer.

Whether we summit - or whether we fall into the abyss - we always accomplish our core objective of developing new understandings.

The Playbook


Your organization's challenges are unique.   Your UX approach must be unique too.

This UX Playbook is a curated list of Activities (called 'plays') that you can use to create better experiences, products and services for your customers.

A UX Playbook contains the entirety of recommended UX solutions that we can bring to the table as a team.  Like the 'plays' that a sports team decides to use in a game, a great coach will adjust what plays are used to suit each specific situation and context.

The Activities that you choose to use from this UX Playbook will depend on the context of your project - and how much time is available to you.

You might use many of the listed Activities (as I do with my teams for full redesign projects that typically last for many weeks) - or you may choose an abbreviated set of Activities for a more focused endeavor that has a shorter time frame. There is no One-Size-Fits-All.

DISCOVER


Phase Objectives:

Explore and understand our stakeholders’ Goals and Success Measures.

Develop a deep and empathetic understanding of our users’ Goals, Needs and Pain Points.
BUSINESS RESEARCH
Step Objectives:  
Understand our Stakeholders’ goals and success measures.

Create a shared understanding of what success looks like for our Product / Project / Team.
Activities Library:  
Go-To Activities

  • Stakeholder Interviews.
  • HEART Metrics and KPIs Review.
  • Microconversions Review.
  • Goals, Signals and Measures Canvas.
OBSERVATION
Step Objectives:  
Detect where key problem areas are in the overall digital experience through Observation.
Activities Library:  
Go-To Activities

  • UR Impressions Testing.   (Qualitative and Quantitative)
    Visual Appeal, Content and Comprehension, Trust & Credibility, Derive Value.
  • UR-UT Existing Product.
    (With Qualitative Feedback)
  • Session Videos & Heat Maps.
  • IA- Card Sorting.
  • IA- Tree Test.
  • Analytics Review.
    (With specific attention to Engagement, Retention and Transaction)
  • Conversion Funnel Analysis.
FEEDBACK
Step Objectives:  
Detect where key problem areas are in the overall digital experience by Listening.
Activities Library:  
Go-To Activities

  • Lightning Talks
    (Explore the Problem Space with Experts)
  • Surveys.
    (With Emphasis on Open-Ended capture and classification)
  • User Interviews.
  • Contextual Enquiries.
  • Customer Support Audit and Interviews.
  • 3rd Party Reviews and Ratings.
  • First Impressions Email.
COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS
Step Objectives:  
Identify and evaluate our place in the marketplace versus competitors.

Perform audits and conduct user testing of competing websites and apps; optimally writing a report that summarizes the competitive landscape and the opportunities that exist within.
Activities Library:  
Go-To Activities

  • Business Model Canvas.
  • Competitive Benchmarking Radars.
    (5 Dimensions, 13 Sub-Vectors, 69 Metrics)
  • 1:1 Feature Audit.
  • Capabilities Analysis.
    (Broader capabilities comparison)
  • Competitive User Flows.
  • Comparative State Diagrams.
    (User Sees.   User Does.)
  • Competitive UR-UT Benchmark Study.
    (UT of 3 Key Tasks against Top 3 Competitors.)
USER INVESTIGATION
Step Objectives:  
Create reliable and realistic representations of our key audience segments.
Activities Library:  
Go-To Activities

  • Brainstorm Product Users.  (New Product)
  • User Canvas.
  • Empathy Map.
  • Persona Development.
JOURNEY INVESTIGATION
Step Objectives:  
Empathetically probe our users' main objectives and journeys, uncovering gaps that may exist in their current experience. refining our lens from the Panorama down to the Partial.
Activities Library:  
Go-To Activities

[The Panorama]
  • Storyboard.
  • Journey Map.
[The Partial]
  • Scenario Map.
  • Task Flows / User Flows.

Double Diamond


The Double Diamond Process allows us to seek out many approaches to a problem.

First we "Diverge" ["Flare"] to generate individual insights, ideas and solutions.

Then we "Converge" ["Focus"] as a group and make decisions.

This repeating process helps us generate a greater number of ideas, and then narrow those down to the winning solution.
